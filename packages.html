Yala National Park (One Day)

Yala is Sri Lanka's most famous national park. Forming a total area of 1268 sq km of scrub, light forest, and grassy plains. Yala is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including elephants, sloth bears, crocodiles, and a significant population of leopards, making it one of the best places in the world to spot these elusive big cats. Accommodation in three-star hotels included.

Madu River Boat Safari (One Day)

The Madu Ganga is a minor watercourse in the Galle District of Sri Lanka, widening into the Madu Ganga Lake at Balapitiya before flowing into the Indian Ocean. It is historically significant as the site of the first upasampada ceremony of the Buddhist Amarapura Nikaya sect in 1803.
